# Benign Pattern: Normal Family Transfer

## Summary
A normal family transfer usually has familiar context, no coercion, and no suspicious lead-up events.

## Typical Channels
- chat
- call
- direct transfer attempt

## Typical Narrative
- family member asks for routine support or reimbursement
- no secrecy
- no fear or legal threats
- no suspicious links or domains
- transfer purpose is understandable and consistent

## Common Benign Signals
- familiar recipient
- clear explanation
- no urgency manipulation
- no suspicious domain
- no authority impersonation
- no transfer immediately after a scam-like call
- recipient name and bank account are expected and consistent

## Why It Is Lower Risk
The transfer context is ordinary and lacks social-engineering indicators.

## Caution
Benign-looking family requests can still be impersonation scams, so context should still be checked if unusual pressure exists.
