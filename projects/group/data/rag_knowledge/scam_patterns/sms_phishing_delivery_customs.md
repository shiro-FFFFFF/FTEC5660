# Scam Pattern: Delivery / Parcel / Customs Phishing

## Summary
This scam pretends to be a courier, parcel company, or customs office. The victim is told there is a delivery issue and is pushed to click a link or pay a small fee.

## Typical Channels
- SMS
- link click
- possible later chat or transfer request

## Typical Narrative
- parcel cannot be delivered
- customs fee or re-delivery fee is needed
- user must act urgently
- a suspicious link is provided

## Common Signals
- parcel, package, customs, delivery
- pay now, final notice, urgent
- shortened or suspicious link
- non-official domain

## Common Social Engineering Tactics
- urgency
- service impersonation
- low-friction payment bait
- phishing

## Why It Is Risky
The scam uses a familiar daily-life pretext and pressures the user to click or pay before thinking carefully.

## Transfer Risk Cues
- transfer or payment request after SMS
- suspicious domain in prior SMS context
- link-based credibility attempt
- later request to pay a new recipient
- recipient name and bank account may not clearly align with the claimed merchant

## Recommended User Action
- do not click the link
- verify the parcel through the official courier website or app
- do not enter card, banking, or identity details

## Recommended Analyst Action
- classify as courier/customs phishing pattern
- preserve message text and domain
- correlate later payment attempts with prior phishing SMS
