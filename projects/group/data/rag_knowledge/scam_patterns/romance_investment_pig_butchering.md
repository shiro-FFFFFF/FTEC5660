# Scam Pattern: Romance / Investment Trust-Building Scam

## Summary
This scam builds trust over time through chat or relationship-style communication, then introduces an investment opportunity or urgent financial transfer.

## Typical Channels
- chat message
- phone call
- occasional SMS
- eventual transfer attempt

## Typical Narrative
- relationship or friendship is built slowly
- scammer appears caring, successful, or financially knowledgeable
- victim is introduced to an investment platform or insider opportunity
- gains may be faked
- victim is eventually asked to transfer larger amounts

## Common Signals
- long trust-building conversation
- guaranteed returns or special insider access
- emotionally persuasive language
- gradual escalation toward transfer
- pressure to act before opportunity closes

## Common Social Engineering Tactics
- trust building
- emotional manipulation
- investment scam framing
- urgency
- authority by false expertise

## Why It Is Risky
The transfer is risky because the victim has been conditioned over time rather than pressured only once. The context across earlier messages is critical.

## Transfer Risk Cues
- prior investment language in chat history
- new recipient
- repeated transfer attempts over time
- language about guaranteed returns, VIP tips, exclusive opportunity
- beneficiary account and recipient name may belong to an unrelated third party

## Recommended User Action
- do not transfer more money
- verify the investment independently
- be cautious if the relationship quickly shifts toward money

## Recommended Analyst Action
- review prior chat context, not just latest message
- classify as trust-building / investment fraud pattern
- escalate repeated transfers to new recipients
