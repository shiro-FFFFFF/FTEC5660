# Scam Pattern: Urgent Transfer Pressure / Payment Redirection

## Summary
This scam pressures the victim into sending money quickly, often after a call or message that creates urgency, confusion, or fear.

## Typical Channels
- phone call
- SMS
- chat
- transfer attempt

## Typical Narrative
- immediate action is required
- recipient is new or changed
- victim is told delay will cause loss, penalty, or danger
- transfer follows shortly after the communication event

## Common Signals
- urgent, immediately, now, final notice, hurry
- new payee or changed account details
- transfer attempt right after call or SMS
- request to keep transfer confidential
- unusual amount

## Common Social Engineering Tactics
- urgency
- isolation
- payment diversion
- fear of consequences

## Why It Is Risky
A transfer that occurs immediately after suspicious communications is often influenced, not independently decided.

## Transfer Risk Cues
- recent call
- recent SMS
- first-time recipient
- elevated or large amount
- beneficiary name mismatch
- beneficiary previously reported
- recipient name / bank account mismatch or close mismatch

## Recommended User Action
- pause before transferring
- verify with trusted contact through official channel
- do not rely on the same message thread for verification

## Recommended Analyst Action
- treat short-gap communication-to-transfer pattern as high signal
- combine cross-channel context before decision
